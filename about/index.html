    Academic/Professional History

    I spent my first 21 years in Singapore where I attended NUS High School of Mathematics and Science. I spent 2 years serving my National Service in the Singapore Armed Forces, commissioning as an Infantry Officer.
     

     

    In 2017 I graduated with a First Class Honors in a BA in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge in the U.K. and was also awarded the Georges Lemaitre Prize1. I then spent 3 months working under Andrew W. Moore as a Research Assistant in the UoC Computer Laboratory, studying how neural networks could be used to classify network traffic data.

     

    In 2019 I graduated with a MS in Mathematics in Finance from New York University, where I was also awarded the Spring 2018 Director’s List2. Whilst at NYU, I worked part time as a Teaching Assistant and also spent 10 weeks working at AQR Capital Management as a Research Intern, doing projects related to factor investing.

     

    I then spent 2.5 years working as a Quantitative Researcher at Bloomberg LP in the QFR team led by Bruno Dupire in New York City. The projects there focused heavily on data visualization, applying mathematical methods to finance and improving various Bloomberg datasets. In my downtime whilst at Bloomberg, I wrote 3 short mathematical papers (now published on SSRN and ResearchGate) inspired by the conversations I had with my colleagues.

     

    I am now working as a Junior Quantitative Trader at Maverick Derivatives in Amsterdam. My work involves building and maintaining trading infrastructure, developing and monitoring trading strategies, and efficient execution of trades.
